## Welcome

Thank you for volunteering to test OneCare — a health management platform that helps patients track medications, vitals, and health documents while enabling secure sharing with their healthcare providers.

We're at the **pre-launch stage**. The platform is functional but has not yet been publicly released. Your feedback will directly shape the product before it reaches real patients and clinicians.

### What Is OneCare?

OneCare is a personal health management app that lets users:

- **Track medications** with schedules and adherence monitoring
- **Log vitals** (blood pressure, glucose, heart rate, weight, temperature, oxygen)
- **Store health documents** in a secure Health Vault with AI-powered summaries
- **Share data with clinicians** through a secure provider sharing system
- **Manage family members'** health from a single account
- **Receive clinician guidance** — instructions and care plans sent directly by their provider

For clinicians, OneCare provides a dashboard to monitor shared patient data, set vital alert thresholds, send clinical guidance, and manage a patient panel.

### What We're Asking of You

We need you to **use the platform as if it were real** — go through the flows relevant to your role, try to break things, and tell us honestly what works, what doesn't, and what confuses you. We value critical feedback above praise.

## Testing Goals

Your feedback will be evaluated against these five goals:

| # | Goal | What We're Looking For |
|---|------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Bug Discovery** | Crashes, broken features, visual glitches, data not saving, error messages, things that don't work as expected |
| 2 | **Design & UX Feedback** | Confusing navigation, unclear layouts, poor visual hierarchy, inconsistent styling, mobile responsiveness issues |
| 3 | **Nomenclature Validation** | Unclear labels, medical terminology that's too jargon-heavy or too simplified, buttons/headings that don't clearly communicate their purpose |
| 4 | **Trust & Credibility Assessment** | Does the platform feel professional? Would you trust it with real health data? What feels off? What's missing to build confidence? |
| 5 | **Regional & Market Fit** | Does the platform work for your country/context? Are currency, units, drug names, or cultural expectations mismatched? |

## How to Report Issues

For **bugs**, please note:
1. **What you were trying to do** (e.g., "I was trying to add a new medication")
2. **What happened instead** (e.g., "The save button did nothing and a red error appeared")
3. **Your device and browser** (e.g., "iPhone 14, Safari" or "Windows laptop, Chrome")
4. **A screenshot** if possible (especially for visual issues)

For **feedback** (design, naming, trust, etc.), use the Google Form linked at the end of this document.

### 4. QA / Technical Testers

**Who this is for:** Testers with technical backgrounds focused on finding bugs, edge cases, and performance issues.

**Context:** Your job is to try to break things. Test unusual inputs, rapid actions, edge cases, and different device/browser combinations.

#### Functional Testing

| Step | Scenario | What to Look For |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| 4.1 | **Form validation:** Try submitting empty forms, extremely long inputs, special characters (é, ñ, 中文), negative numbers, dates in the past/far future | Do forms handle bad input gracefully? Are error messages helpful and specific? |
| 4.2 | **Navigation:** Use browser back/forward buttons, bookmark pages, open links in new tabs, deep-link directly to inner pages | Does routing work correctly? Do pages load properly when accessed directly? |
| 4.3 | **Auth edge cases:** Try accessing protected pages without logging in, switch between patient/clinician accounts, test password reset flow | Are redirects correct? Is session handling reliable? Does data leak between accounts? |
| 4.4 | **Data operations:** Add then immediately edit/delete a medication, quickly mark multiple doses, log vitals with boundary values (e.g., BP 300/200, glucose 0) | Do CRUD operations work reliably? Are there race conditions? |

#### Cross-Device & Browser Testing

| Step | Scenario | What to Look For |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| 4.5 | Test on **mobile** (iOS Safari, Android Chrome) | Does the layout adapt properly? Are touch targets large enough? Can you access all features? |
| 4.6 | Test on **desktop** (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge) | Any browser-specific rendering issues? Do modals, dropdowns, and tooltips work correctly? |
| 4.7 | Test on a **slow connection** (use browser DevTools → Network → Slow 3G) | Do loading states appear? Does the app remain usable? Do images/charts load gracefully? |
| 4.8 | Test with a **small screen** (resize browser to ~320px width) | Does content overflow? Are buttons still tappable? Is text still readable? |

#### Accessibility & Error Handling

| Step | Scenario | What to Look For |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| 4.9 | **Keyboard navigation:** Try using the app with only a keyboard (Tab, Enter, Escape) | Can you reach all interactive elements? Are focus states visible? Can you close modals with Escape? |
| 4.10 | **Screen reader check** (if you have one available) | Are images described? Are form fields labeled? Is content order logical? |
| 4.11 | **Error recovery:** Disconnect internet briefly during a save operation, then reconnect | Does the app recover? Is data lost? Is there a meaningful error message? |
| 4.12 | **Performance:** Navigate through the app rapidly, open several heavy pages (Vitals charts, Dashboard) | Any noticeable lag? Do charts render smoothly? Is scrolling smooth with lots of data? |

## Testing Tips

- **Be specific.** "The button didn't work" is less helpful than "The 'Save Medication' button on the Add Medication page did nothing when I tapped it on iPhone Safari."
- **Note your expectations.** If something confused you, tell us what you *expected* to happen.
- **First impressions matter.** Don't skip the fresh sign-up — your initial reactions before you learn the system are incredibly valuable.
- **Nothing is too small.** Typos, misaligned icons, awkward wording — we want to hear about it all.
- **Compare to what you know.** If you use other health apps or clinical tools, tell us how OneCare compares.
