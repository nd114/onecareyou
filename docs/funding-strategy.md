# OneCare Funding Strategy

## Why "Be Careful Who You Take Money From" Is True

### 1. Mission Dilution
Health-tech investors often push for aggressive monetization — selling data, ads, paywalling critical health features. OneCare's value prop is patient-owned data and free access to core features. The wrong investor will demand you paywall vitals or sell anonymized data to pharma, destroying the trust proposition that makes OneCare different.

### 2. Premature Scaling Pressure
VC money comes with growth expectations (10x in 18 months). For a health platform launching in Nigeria, you need time to build trust with communities, iterate on clinical workflows, and validate product-market fit. Forced scaling leads to cutting safety corners in a domain where safety is non-negotiable.

### 3. Geographic Misalignment
Most US/EU health-tech VCs don't understand the African healthcare market. They'll push US-first strategies, ignore offline-first needs, and undervalue community-driven adoption — the exact strategy that will make OneCare succeed.

### 4. Control Loss
Taking a large seed round typically means giving up 15-25% equity + board seats. At this stage with one developer, that's giving away significant control before proving the model. Early-stage health-tech founders who lose board control often find their product vision overridden by investors chasing short-term metrics.

### 5. Regulatory Risk Transfer
Health-tech investors know HIPAA/regulatory liability. Some structure deals to shift compliance burden entirely to the founder. If a breach occurs, the founder absorbs the legal risk while investors are protected. Read term sheets carefully.

## Recommended Funding Strategy

### Phase 0: Bootstrap + Micro-Grants (Now – Month 3)

**Target: $25K–$100K (non-dilutive)**

Continue self-funding operational costs (~$900–$2,050/month per launch plan budget).

**Grant Opportunities:**
- **Google for Startups Africa** — up to $100K in credits + cash
- **Techstars Lagos** or **Y Combinator** — $500K standard deal (if applicable)
- **Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation Grand Challenges** — health innovation grants, $100K–$500K
- **USAID Digital Health** — grants for African health-tech
- **Africa Health Business** — grants for health platforms
- **Savannah Fund** — East Africa-focused, $25K–$500K

**Why this phase matters:** Non-dilutive funding preserves 100% equity while validating the product. Even small grants provide credibility for later fundraising.

### Phase 1: Angel Round (Months 3–6)

**Target: $50K–$150K**

**Prerequisites:** 50+ registered patients, 5+ active clinicians, basic retention data.

**Sources:**
- **Nigerian angel investors** — Lagos Angel Network, Ventures Platform
- **Diaspora angels** — Nigerian professionals in US/UK health systems who understand both markets
- **Doctor-investors** — practicing clinicians who see the problem firsthand (also become advisors and champions)

**Terms:** SAFE notes, no board seats, 10-15% equity cap

**Use of Funds:**
- Hire Community Growth Lead ($100–150/month)
- Hire Content Specialist ($200–500/month)
- 6 months of operational runway
- Social media ad budget ($100–200/month)

### Phase 2: Pre-Seed (Months 6–12)

**Target: $250K–$500K**

**Prerequisites:** 300+ patients, 25+ clinicians, $700+ MRR, expansion to 2-3 countries.

**Sources:**
- **Future Africa** — pan-African pre-seed, $50K–$250K
- **Ingressive Capital** — West Africa focus
- **Flat6Labs** — MENA/Africa
- **Global Health Innovative Technology Fund (GHIT)** — specifically for health platforms
- **Impact investors** — Omidyar Network, Acumen (mission-aligned)

**Terms:** Priced round at $2M–$4M valuation, 15-20% equity

**Use of Funds:**
- Second developer (full-time/contract)
- Sales team (commission-based)
- Kenya/Ghana expansion (Country Manager)
- Infrastructure scaling

### Phase 3: Seed (Months 12–18)

**Target: $1M–$3M**

**Prerequisites:** Proven unit economics, strong retention, growing MRR.

**Sources:**
- **TLcom Capital** — Africa-focused VC
- **Norrsken** — Impact-focused VC
- **Algebra Ventures** — MENA/Africa
- Health-tech focused VCs with Africa experience

**Only pursue if:** Growth metrics justify it AND strategic alignment is confirmed with the investor's portfolio and mission.

## Key Funding Principles

1. **Non-dilutive first** — Exhaust grants and competitions before giving away equity
2. **Aligned investors only** — Health background, Africa experience, patient-first ethos
3. **Small checks, many backers** — 10 angels at $10K each is safer than 1 investor at $100K
4. **Revenue before fundraising** — Even $500/month MRR makes fundraising 10x easier
5. **Keep control** — Maintain >70% founder equity through pre-seed minimum
6. **No data monetization deals** — Reject any investor who suggests selling patient data
7. **Read term sheets carefully** — Watch for anti-dilution clauses, liquidation preferences, and compliance liability shifts

## Red Flags in Potential Investors

- Pushing to monetize patient data or add advertising
- No experience with African markets
- Demanding board seats at angel stage
- Portfolio includes companies that compete with patient interests (pharma data brokers, insurance analytics)
- Pressure to abandon Africa-first strategy for US-first
- Unrealistic growth expectations (10x in 12 months for a health platform)
- Vague about their value-add beyond capital

## Green Flags in Potential Investors

- Background in healthcare or public health
- Experience with African startups or markets
- Portfolio includes patient-empowerment tools
- Willing to invest via SAFE notes without board seats
- Connections to clinicians, hospitals, or health systems
- Patient about timeline — understands health-tech trust-building takes time
- Offers operational support (hiring, partnerships, regulatory guidance)
